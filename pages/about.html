A Little About Me!

Hello! I want to thank you for stopping by my about page and learning a little about me! I'm going to keep this professional by detailing my skills and experience to offer you a broader view of my capabilities beyond what I can do inside a resume.

I'll start with my educational background! I graduated from Frostburg State University with a Bachelor of Science degree in Law & Society with a minor in Graphic Design. My original plans for a career revolved around law enforcement. However, graduating in the midst of the Covid-19 pandemic and many of the events surrounding law enforcement I became very uncertain about this career path. I started working full time for the family construction business full time after I graduated. After a few years I decided to re-enroll in school to focus on a career within computer science. January, 2022 I began the process of earning a second bachelor's degree from University of Maryland Global Campus while remaining employed full time. I am on track to graduate UMGC in August 2023!

My coursework at UMGC provided me many opportunities to learn a variety of skills pertaining to web development, software engineering and graphic design. A few of these skills include:

  • HTML5 & CSS programming languages
  • JavaScript (including the jQuery, Ajax and JSON libraries)
  • LESS (CSS preprocessor)
  • UX and UI principles
  • Adobe Creative Suite (Adobe Photoshop, Adobe Illustrator, Adobe XD)

However, I discovered while learning in school that I developed a passion for coding. Often times hours would feel like minutes while I was developing a website or troubleshooting a bug. So in addition to my official coursework during the intermissions between semesters I learned more languages and tools from online coding bootcamps like freecodecamp and Codecademy. I supplemented my existing knowledge on several languages learned in class as well as learned others such as:

  • React.js JavaScript library
  • Git & GitHub
  • PHP

Other langauges I want to learn and will pursue in the future include:

  • C++
  • Python
  • C#

As you can see I know a lot but also recognize I can always learn more!

Now for a little about my working experience. Currently I am lacking relevant work experience within the computer science industry though I am actively pursing internship and entry level opportunities when I find them. Though I have been working for a large portion of my life. I started working summers for the family business at the age of 13 and after going to college took a job in the student union building at Frostburg State. These working experiences have provided me the opportunity to learn valuable skills that are essential in all industries.

  • Written and verbal communication
  • Time management
  • Adaptability
  • The ability to learn from and accept constructive criticism
  • Leadership
These are just a few of the soft skills I have learned from my previous work experiences. In fact while working for the student union I earned several awards including Frostburg State's first ever Student Employee of the Year award. The student union's Manager of the Year award. I even was awarded my local fraternity chapter's Abraham Lincoln award for inspiring positive growth and development in younger brothers.

I could keep going on what makes me unique and why I would be a valuable addition to a development or engineering team. If you've made it to the end here I want to thank you for taking the time and hope that I have provided a little more context to my resume!
